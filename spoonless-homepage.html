From My Kitchen

A lifelong obsession that finally has somewhere to go.

I've been eating peanut butter my whole life — from packed pb&j sandwiches as a kid to a slightly more elevated version as an adult. I literally signed up for peanut butter of the month club with my first paycheck, and I truly think a pan fried peanut butter banana sandwich would be my last meal.

I love peanut butter so much that I knew it had to be a part of my most recent birthday. I invited my friends over for brunch on a Sunday morning, and that's where the idea for Spoonless came. I'd microwaved store-bought peanut butter, poured it into a squeeze bottle I ordered from Amazon, and suddenly everyone was crowding into my kitchen drizzling it over pancakes, waffles, fruit, and honestly anything they could find. Then it hardened and the moment was over.

I couldn't stop thinking "wow, if someone could get this right." Turns out that person was me. Spoonless isn't a business idea — it's a lifelong obsession that finally has somewhere to go.

The birthday brunch that started it all.
